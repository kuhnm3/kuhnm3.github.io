IdeaHack

As a student at the Startup Institute, I was assigned to a group of 6 classmates to particpate in IdeaHack. We were given just under 24 hours to help the new startup, Boston Public Market, tell their story.

The Challenges:

1. How can they use technology to:

  • --Communicate local
  • --Differentiate from competitors
  • --Share their vendors' unique stories
  • --Create a virtual community that share recipes, cooking tips, best practices for cooking seasonality, etc.

2. What does their long-term engagement strategy for customers look like?:

  • --Maximize impact from newletter/social media/outreach
  • --Identify and move on new ways of engaging customers
  • --Create a feel that supports their brand and long term goals
  • --Draw customers to the Market (promotions, programming or otherwise)

Our theme:

Change #1

Our first change was to redo the homepage of their website to give people a better understanding of what they do. Even if people only went to their homepage, we wanted them to gain the experience of what Public Market is about.

Change #2

Our next change was to add a monthly feature of one of the farmers at Boston Public Market. Each month they would feature a different farmer. This would allow people to get to know the vendors on a more personal level.

Change #3

Our third change was to add a membership program. One key parts of the membership would be the meal plan. Every week the members would get a few meals, each with a reciple and list on ingredients to pick up at Boston Public Market.
